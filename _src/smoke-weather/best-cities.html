Frequently asked questions

How are the cities ranked?

Each metro gets a 0-100 smoke score for today's weather, and the cities are ranked from highest to lowest. The score starts at 100 and subtracts penalties for rain, wind and gusts, cold mornings, hot afternoons, and stall risk. A higher score means better conditions to run the smoker. See the methodology page for the full formula.

How often is the ranking updated?

The ranking refreshes daily from live weather forecasts, so the best and toughest cities change as conditions move. It reflects today's score for each metro; open a city's page for its full 7-day outlook.

Which city is the best place to smoke meat?

There's no single answer: it changes every day with the weather. A humid Gulf Coast day that hurts brisket in Houston can rank below a clear, calm day in Denver. Check the live ranking above for today's best city, then confirm with that city's 7-day forecast before you commit.
