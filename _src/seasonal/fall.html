Fall Smoke Days

Fall is the strongest cook window of the year for most regions: cooler air, lower dew points, calm jet stream, and the social demand of tailgate season layered on top.

The cleanest cooking window of the year

Fall, roughly mid-September through mid-November in most of the country, produces the best stretch of cook weather most regions see. The summer humidity peels back from the south, the winter cold has not yet arrived in the north, and the jet stream sits relatively quiet between the two seasonal patterns. Dew points fall steadily week over week, which means the wet-bulb-driven summer stall starts to behave again.

The other defining feature of fall smoking is demand. Football season, neighborhood tailgates, family gatherings, and holiday meals all stack into the fall calendar, which is why competition pitmasters cluster their events in September and October. Frequently asked questions

Why is fall the best season to smoke?
Fall combines the variables that all favor a clean cook: daytime highs settle into the 50s-70s across most regions, dew points drop steadily from late September into November, the jet stream is not yet aggressive enough for routine frontal-passage gusts, and afternoon storm cells are far less common than in summer. The wet-bulb temperature is in the friendly zone for long stalls. Most pitmasters cook more brisket in October than any other month.
Is tailgate season special for smoking?
Tailgate season (roughly September through January) drives the largest social demand for big cooks of the year: football Saturdays and Sundays mean ribs, pulled pork, brisket, and wings on a scale most weekends don't see. The fall weather supports those cooks more reliably than any other season, which is why competition BBQ events cluster in September and October.
When should I start thinking about winter prep?
Late fall (November in most regions) is when an open-firebox offset starts to feel the fuel-rate inflation that winter brings. If you're planning to keep cooking through December and January, late fall is the right time to clean and check your insulated kamado or pellet rig, stock dry wood, and decide whether the upcoming winter calls for a cooker upgrade.
